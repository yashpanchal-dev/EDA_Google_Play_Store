# 📱 Google Play Store Data Analysis (EDA Project)

This project performs **Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)** on the Google Play Store dataset to understand app trends, user preferences, and factors influencing app success such as ratings, installs, categories, and pricing.

---

## 📌 Project Overview

The Google Play Store hosts millions of applications across various categories. This project analyzes app data to uncover insights about:

- app ratings and reviews  
- installs and popularity  
- pricing (free vs paid apps)  
- category performance  
- content rating distribution  

The goal is to help developers and businesses understand **what makes an app successful**.

---

## 🛠 Tools & Technologies Used

- Python  
- Pandas  
- NumPy  
- Matplotlib  
- Seaborn  
- Jupyter Notebook  

---

## 📊 Key Analysis Performed

### 1️⃣ Data Cleaning
- Removed missing and null values  
- Handled inconsistent data formats (Installs, Price, Size)  
- Converted columns into proper numeric types  

### 2️⃣ App Ratings Analysis
- Analyzed distribution of app ratings  
- Identified highest-rated categories  

### 3️⃣ Installs & Popularity Analysis
- Examined apps with highest installs  
- Identified most popular categories  

### 4️⃣ Category-wise Analysis
- Compared number of apps across categories  
- Identified categories with maximum competition  

### 5️⃣ Free vs Paid Apps Analysis
- Compared performance of free vs paid apps  
- Analyzed price distribution  

### 6️⃣ Content Rating Analysis
- Analyzed target audience (Everyone, Teen, Mature, etc.)  
- Distribution of apps across content ratings  

### 7️⃣ Reviews & Correlation Analysis
- Relationship between **reviews, installs, and ratings**  
- Identified patterns influencing app success  

---

## 📈 Key Insights

- Most apps fall in the **4.0–4.5 rating range**  
- **Free apps dominate** the Play Store and have significantly higher installs  
- Categories like **Family, Game, and Tools** have the highest number of apps  
- Apps with higher reviews tend to have **higher installs and better ratings**  
- Paid apps are fewer but can generate value in niche segments  

---

## ✅ Conclusion

This project demonstrates how **Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)** can be used to extract meaningful insights from real-world app marketplace data.

The analysis highlights that **free apps dominate the Google Play Store ecosystem**, especially in high-demand categories such as **Games, Family, and Tools**. It also reveals a strong relationship between **user engagement (reviews) and app success**, where apps with higher reviews tend to achieve more installs and better ratings.

Additionally, the study shows that while paid apps are fewer, they can perform well in niche markets with targeted audiences.

Overall, this project showcases practical skills in **data cleaning, feature transformation, visualization, and insight generation using Python**, helping understand key factors that influence app performance and user behavior.
